2023-01-26 C/2022 E3 (ZTF)

Another attempt at C/2022 E3 (ZTF). This is only 6 days later than the previous attempt, but the comet is closing rapidly and changes every day, so I'll take any attempt at this once in a lifetime experience.
  • Location
    • Name: Movatn, Oslo, Norway
    • Lat Long: 60.039, 10.815
    • Datetime: January 26th, ~20:00h
    • Bortle Scale: Class 5
  • Gear
    • Camera: Canon EOS 700D
    • Lens: Pentax Takumar 300mm f/4
    • Tracking: None/Tripod
  • Exposure
    • Aperture: f/4.0
    • Focal Length: 300mm
    • Shutter Speed: 3/1s
    • ISO: 3200
  • Processing
    • Light Frames: 200
    • Stacker: DeepSkyStacker
    • Total Exposure: 10min 0s
    • Editor: GIMP

Result

Quite different result than the previous attempt. I gave my Canon EOS 700D a go this time, and it is interesting to see how it compares to my usual Fujifilm X-T1 in how it handles noise and color.

While the comet is much closer and exposed for 6 times as long, not much more detail is resolved in the tail. There also seems to be more noise in the form of strong diagonal banding. This is a huge tradeoff with the Canon, and something the ISO-invariant Fujifilm handles much better in my experience.
