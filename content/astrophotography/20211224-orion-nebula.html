2021-12-24 Orion Nebula

My first attempt at photographing Messier 42, better known as the Orion Nebula.
  • Location
    • Name: Reksteren, Vestland Norway
    • Lat Long: 60.04, 05.42
    • Datetime: December 24th, ~01:00h
    • Bortle Scale: Class 4
  • Gear
    • Camera: Canon Eos 700D
    • Lens: Included Kit Lens
  • Exposure
    • Aperture: f/5.6
    • Focal Length: 55mm
    • Shutter Speed: 5/1s
  • Stack
    • Software: Sequator
    • Light Frames: 80
    • Total Exposure: 6min 40s

Processing

The stacked image has been manually processed in GIMP using a color-preserving arcsinh stretch pipeline.
  1. Light pollution were subtracted from each channel individually.
  2. Blackpoint adjusted until, but not including, clipping data.
  3. Color-preserving arcsinh stretch.
  4. Weak contrast S-curve adjustment targeting faint stars.

Result

Despire glaring issues like the strong chromatic aberration and slightly out-of-focus stars, I'm pretty happy with the result. I didn't dare subtract more of the light pollution gradient as the histogram overlap between the nebula and noise were quite severe. Taking a closer look the (here tiny) Great Orion Nebula is clearly visible with it's pink-ish haze. Straight above that one can barely see the Running Man Nebula. Barely visible is also parts of the Flame Nebula in Alnitak's top left, something I didn't expect to see. I have included the B-V Index of a few stars for reference for color reference.
  • Alnilam: -0.19, Blue-White
  • Alnitak: -0.09, Blue-White
  • V1375 Ori: +1.30, Orange-Red
  • 31 Ori: +1.58, Redder
Values have been sourced from Stellarium.
